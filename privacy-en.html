Privacy Policy

For the "DailyReminder" iOS app. Last updated: June 25, 2026

1. Controller and contact

The controller is the person or entity named in the imprint/legal notice. Until the imprint is finalized, you can contact support at Dailyreminder@hotmail.com.

2. Data stored locally by the app

DailyReminder stores your reminders, categories, schedules, completed days, language, weekly goal, challenge status, starter routine, widget snapshot data, and legal consent locally on your device. Without iCloud sync, this data is not automatically sent to our own servers.

For troubleshooting, the app also stores a small local event log, for example whether a reminder was created, a backup was exported, or a purchase screen was opened. Reminder contents are not written to this event log.

3. Notifications

If you allow notifications, the app uses Apple's local iOS notifications to remind you about your own reminders. Delivery depends on your iOS settings.

App Shortcuts/Siri features may locally create reminders or mark reminders as completed when you actively use those features.

4. Ads and consent

The free version may display ads. Google Mobile Ads and Google User Messaging Platform may be used. Depending on your region and consent, device information, advertising identifiers, usage data, consent status, and technical data may be processed. Premium removes ads in the app while Premium is active.

5. Premium, subscriptions, and in-app purchases

Premium features, subscriptions, and purchase restoration may be processed through RevenueCat and the Apple App Store. Purchase status, product ID, app user ID, device information, and transaction data may be processed. Payment information is processed by Apple, not directly by DailyReminder.

6. Support, sharing, and diagnostics

If you contact support or share diagnostics, we process the information you actively send, such as app version, language, notification status, reminder count, weekly goal, device model, iOS version, and the local event log.

7. Backup and export

If you use the backup/export feature, the app creates a JSON file containing your reminders and history. This file is only created when you actively start an export. If you share it or store it externally, you are responsible for the recipient or storage location.

8. Open source licenses

The app includes a local overview of open source licenses for the libraries used. This overview is generated from the included packages during the build.

9. App Store privacy disclosures

App Store privacy details and privacy manifest information must be maintained for release. The app uses local storage such as UserDefaults for app functionality. If ads, subscriptions, or external SDKs are active, the App Store privacy disclosures must match the actually enabled providers and data flows.

10. Legal bases

Where GDPR applies, processing is based on your consent, providing app functionality, fulfilling a contract for Premium, or legitimate interests such as security, troubleshooting, and product improvement, depending on the feature.

11. Your rights

Depending on applicable law, you may request access, correction, deletion, restriction, portability, objection, and withdrawal of consent. Locally stored app data can be deleted in the app or by removing the app from your device.

12. Changes

This Privacy Policy may be updated when features, providers, or legal requirements change. Before App Store release, the final version should be legally reviewed.
